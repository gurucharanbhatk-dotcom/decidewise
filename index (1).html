⚖️ Manual Mode — Step by Step

1

Choose a Template (or start blank)

Click any template chip like "Job vs Job" or "Buy vs Rent" to instantly load relevant factors. Templates save you time — all the important factors are pre-filled. You can always add or remove factors after loading a template.

2

Name Your Decision

Give your decision a clear title like "Infosys vs TCS Offer" or "Bangalore vs Hyderabad Move". This helps you identify it when you save and revisit later.

3

Add Your Options (2 to 6)

Add the things you're comparing. For a job decision: "Offer A — TCS" and "Offer B — Infosys". For a city move: "Bangalore", "Pune", "Hyderabad". Be specific — good names help you think clearly.

4

Set Factors & Weights

Factors are what matters to you. Weight is how much each factor matters relative to the others. See the weight guide below. Score each option 1–5 stars on every factor.

5

Click "Calculate Decision Score"

The engine calculates a weighted total score for each option, shows you a comparison bar chart, explains why the winner wins, and suggests your next action.

⚙️ How Weights Work — Complete Guide

Weights tell the engine how important each factor is to you. A factor with weight 10 matters 10× more than a factor with weight 1. This is what makes your result personalised, not generic.

1–3

Low importance

Nice to have, but not a dealbreaker. Example: office aesthetics, brand name prestige.

4–6

Medium importance

Matters, but you could compromise. Example: company culture, learning opportunities.

7–9

High importance

Very important to you. Would heavily influence your decision. Example: salary, location.

10

Deal-breaker / Must-have

Non-negotiable. If an option scores low here, it likely loses. Example: job security if you have dependents.

💡 Pro tip: Don't give everything a weight of 10 — that defeats the purpose. Force yourself to rank what truly matters most. The more honest your weights, the more useful your result.

Example: Job Decision Weights

You have a family, need stability, and care about growth. Set weights like: Job Security → 10, Salary → 9, Growth → 8, Work-Life Balance → 7, Company Culture → 5, Location → 4, Brand Name → 2.

Now score each job 1–5 on each factor. The math handles the rest.

🤖 AI Mode — How to Get the Best Results

!

Be specific. Give context. Mention your priorities.

The more detail you give, the better the AI recommendation. Don't just say "2 job offers" — say "Job A pays ₹18 LPA in Mumbai but I'm from Chennai, Job B pays ₹14 LPA remote-first. I have elderly parents and prefer stability over money."

📝 Good AI prompt format: Situation → Options → Your constraints/priorities → What you're confused about
